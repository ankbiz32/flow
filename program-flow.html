Free To Learn Our Way

Program for students

Overview

Free To Learn Our Way began with an idea of a world, a vision of kinder kids, emotionally learned beings, socially inclusive students with a sense of community spirit, humane communication, happier folks, facilitating We(s) in the world of I(s) and of people in touch with their inner world with all of its landscapes, flowers, dreams and power. Our purpose is to facilitate a space for children where they are free to learn their own way.

Free To Learn Our Way intends to:

  • ✔
    Give the students a space to explore 10 different areas of learning and life skills with 10 different modules that can very well be integrated in their educational journey.
  • ✔
    Focus on experiential hands on learning as the cornerstone of the entire program.
  • ✔
    Evoke responses from the students over statements made, making the teaching a 2-way process.
  • ✔
    Nudging the students to self-reflect on the teachings by exploring and developing insights. This in a way helps them to find an anchor within themselves.
  • ✔
    Encourage students to explore their emotional and social selves.
  • ✔
    Ensuring smooth delivery of the program by well-inducted Flow trainers assigned to the school called ‘Flow buddy’.
  • ✔
    Empowering the students with tools and models that they can carry with themselves for life like Johari Window and Drama Triangle to name a few.
  • ✔
    Focus on transforming the classroom into a safe space to share and learn through practices like a sharing circle, group discussions and Think Pair Share (TPS) to name a few.
  • ✔
    Complement the existing framework of holistic education by a program module created by research of over 1 year.
  • ✔
    Facilitate the process of empowering students to be better and whole human beings.

Our Approach: AIR

Whilst the skills acquired through academic study are in constant use, it is possible for the concepts learned to sometimes exist in the mind alone if not applied and experienced, where they can eventually cease to exist.
Free to Learn Our Way is different, as it relates directly to experiences that students will have in their everyday lives. It is possible to teach all about the concepts that we have created in our program to students, but that would be to miss the point. We believe that the teaching must have experience as its primary aim: we should be teaching the students not just ‘to know’ but also how to be and to do.
In order for this to work, we have to get the students to experience it. Our approach helps us to do it, which is:

Awareness
(Notice)
Intervention
(Act and experience)
Reflect
(Self evaluation)
